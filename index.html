Why London?

London, historical, cultural and economic megacity

London's history spans over two millennia, from its Roman founding as Londinium in 43 AD to its current status as a global capital. Initially a settlement on the River Thames, it was later influenced by Anglo-Saxons, Vikings, and Normans. The city experienced growth, plague, fire, and the rise and fall of the British Empire, ultimately becoming a megacity and one of the most famous cities in the world.

Today, London is a vibrant metropolis, a major economic, transportation, and cultural hub, and a testament to its resilience and enduring legacy. Its history is woven into the fabric of its streets, buildings, and institutions, making it a city with a rich past and a dynamic present.

My favorite museums in London

Tower of London

The Tower of London, officially His Majesty's Royal Palace and Fortress of the Tower of London, is a historic citadel and castle on the north bank of the River Thames in central London. Throughout its long history, it was a secure fortress, a royal palace, and an infamous prison. Kings and queens demonstrated their power from here, shaping society and influencing our world.

Address:

London, EC3N 4AB.

What I like about it

There is a rich history of spies, political games and treason in the Tower of London and it was fun to learn about it all under a watchful eye of the Tower's Ravens.

British Museum

The British Museum is a public museum dedicated to human history, art and culture located in London. Its permanent collection of eight million works is the largest in the world and it is one of the most famous museums in the world.

Address:

Great Russell St, London WC1B 3DG

What I like about it

Great highlights to see in this grandiose museum, like Egyptian mummies, statues from ancient Greece and Rome, the famous Rosetta stone and many others.

Natural History Museum of London

The Natural History Museum in London is a museum that exhibits a vast range of specimens from various segments of natural history.

Address:

Cromwell Rd, South Kensington, London SW7 5BD

What I like about it

The museum is great fun and has wonderful exhibitions, some of them interactive. You get to learn about our planet, human evolution, dinosaurs, all different kinds of animals and wonders of nature. Great entertainment for the whole family!
